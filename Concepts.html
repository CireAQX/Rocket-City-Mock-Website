This page explains key football terms and position abbreviations used throughout this website.

Football Terms

Interception (INT)

When a defensive player catches a pass that was intended for an offensive receiver. An interception is a turnover that gives the ball to the defense. A pick-six is an interception that is returned for a touchdown.

Sack

When a defensive player tackles the quarterback behind the line of scrimmage before he can throw the ball. Sacks result in a loss of yards for the offense and are a key measure of pass-rushing ability.

Tackle For Loss (TFL)

When a defensive player tackles an offensive player behind the line of scrimmage on a running play. A tackle for loss results in negative yardage for the offense. Sacks are a specific type of TFL.

Forced Fumble (FF)

When a defensive player knocks the ball loose from an offensive player's hands. A forced fumble creates a loose ball that either team can recover.

Pass Deflection (PD)

When a defensive player knocks down or tips a pass without catching it. A pass deflection prevents a completion and is a key stat for cornerbacks and safeties.

Reception (REC)

When an offensive player successfully catches a pass from the quarterback. A reception counts as a completed pass for the quarterback and a catch for the receiver.

Yards (YDS)

The total distance a player gains with the ball, measured in yards. Yards can be gained by rushing (running the ball) or receiving (catching passes). The field is 100 yards long.

Redshirt

When a player sits out an entire season to preserve one year of eligibility. College players have four years of eligibility. By redshirting, the player can practice and develop without using a year, then play four full seasons after.

This page explains the key football and dynasty mode concepts referenced throughout this website.

1. Development Trait

Every player has a development trait that determines how quickly they improve: Normal, Impact, Star, or Elite. A Normal player improves slowly, while an Elite player can jump multiple OVR points in a single offseason. This trait is critical for recruiting and deciding who gets playing time.

2. Redshirt

A redshirt is when a player sits out an entire season to preserve one year of eligibility. College players have four years of eligibility. By redshirting a freshman, the player can practice and develop without using a year, then play four full seasons after. Common for young players not ready to contribute immediately.

3. Depth Chart

The depth chart is the coaching staff's ranked list of players at every position. The first player listed is the starter, the second is the backup. Depth chart decisions are based on OVR, game performance, development potential, and team needs. Managing the depth chart determines who plays, who develops, and where the team is vulnerable.

4. Player Archetype

An archetype is the specific role or style of a player within their position group. A quarterback can be a Field General (pocket passer), Improviser (mobile), or Scrambler (run-first). A wide receiver might be a Deep Threat, Slot Receiver, or Possession Receiver. The archetype determines which attributes matter most and how the player fits the scheme.

5. Recruiting

Recruiting is the process of convincing high school players to commit to your program. In dynasty mode, recruiting happens throughout the season. The coaching staff identifies recruits based on position needs, star ratings, archetype fit, and development potential. Recruiting is the foundation of long-term program building.

6. Scheme Fit

Scheme fit refers to how well a player's skills and archetype match the team's offensive or defensive system. A spread offense needs fast receivers and a mobile QB, while a pro-style offense might need a strong-armed pocket passer and physical tight ends. Better scheme fit means better performance and faster development.
